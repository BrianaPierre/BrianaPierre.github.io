The A.S.D. unit at NMB has 3 functional levels in the classroom. A high group of students, a middle group of students, and a low group that are nonverbal. As of 2021-2022 school year, students work in the green house daily. On Mondays and Tuesdays the High functioning group of students go to the green house. This group performs: planting different species of vegetables and herbs: (tomatoes, bell peppers, onions, rosemary etc.), watering the plats and different other simple activities to maintain the green house in good conditions.

On Wednesdays, the low functioning group of students go to the green house. They mainly water the plants and with the help of the school- teacher assistants pick up the fruits and vegetables.

On Thursdays and Fridays, the middle functioning group of students go to the green house. This group with little help from the school assistants participates in all activities to maintain the green house.

For the 2021-2022 school year, the students grew tomatoes, onions, garlic, bell peppers, potatoes, basil, rosemary, aloe, papaya plants. In November of 2021, we had over 20 tomato plants. We were harvesting up to 5 pounds of tomatoes a day. After January most of the plants had given all, they can bear. We had to plant new tomato plants in January.

In March of 2022 during the spring blossom in South Florida, the plants were about 3 feet tall, and they were about to bloom, and several insect predators appeared in the garden. The tomato plants were left with no leaves. After doing research on how to protect plants from insect infestation, the students discovered that the tomato plants where being eaten by caterpillars. These are the tomato long horn caterpillars. The students plan for next year to build bird houses and a bird bath to bring in birds which will help to eliminate the insect infestation.

To keep all other insects away from the garden, ASD students apply a solution made up of dawn soap, water, and vegetable oil. To prepare the solution, they use empty plastic bottle containers and mix water with 30% dawn soap and 10% vegetable oil. This mixture keeps the white flies away. The tip they perform is to spray under the leaves to keep the bugs away.

For next year, the student's main goal is to bring the fruits and vegetables to the chef who teaches at NMB, and he will use the organic produce in the school kitchen. ASD students want to go from the garden to kitchen to the plate. Their plan is to grow enough vegetables and fruits as an additional food supply for the school cafeteria.

For the 2022-2023 school year, the students are aiming to grow plants using fertilizers from worm excrement. The aim of this experiment is for ASD students to observe how well the vegetables would grow with a variety of fertilizer while also helping ASD students with mental wellness.

As of November 2022, the ASD students have provided the Chef with fresh organic plants such as basil, tomatoes, and garlic to be used in the cooking lab. These plants were grown by the ASD students in the greenhouse, and lets the ASD students be able to provide the Chef with fresh ingredients.
